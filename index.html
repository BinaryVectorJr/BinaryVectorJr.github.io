ABOUT ME

Hello there! My name is Angshuman Mazumdar. I'm 21 years old and hail from a small city called Guwahati, which is in the state of Assam in India. I am currently doing my Bachelors from Vellore Institute of Technology (VIT) in Electronics and Communication Engineering. I am an aspiring game developer, with most of my skills focussed on level design. I am also an amateur concept artist and graphic designer. I like to conceptualize new ideas, and create new stories. For me, video games are the perfect amalgamation of engineering, design, art, and philosophy. They are the perfect platform to publish an idea, or a concept, due to the fact that out of all entertainment media, this is the only one that offers some degree of interactivity and flexibility. I feel that this field has so much potential, in so many sectors, and we should properly utilize the resources currently available to maximize its potential.

I have always been fascinated by the concept of interactive media, especially video games. Not just video games, but the industry itself - the conceptualization, development and execution processes that happen when a new video game is made. My first interaction with video games was way back around 2001-02 when I was around 5-6 years old. Just like every other kid from my generation, Super Mario Brothers was the thing to have at that time, and I used to regularly visit my friend's house, just to play it. And I loved it. I knew that when I grew up, I just had to be involved amidst all this, in one way or the other. From there, till this date, I haven't let that enthusiasm simmer down. One thing that always excites me is how the developers have changed the concept of video games - from being a simple form of entertainment, to something that delivers an other-worldly experience and brings communities together - the entire landscape has changed. And I do believe that there are still many ideas and stories in this world that have not yet been explored. And that is why I want to elevate this industry, in a way that tells the public, that even though video games are primarily considered as forms of entertainment, they can be so much more.
